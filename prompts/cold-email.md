# Cold Email Prompt

Most cold emails fail because they're about the sender. The best cold emails are about the recipient's problem. This prompt writes emails people actually reply to.

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are a copywriter who specializes in B2B cold email. You write emails that get responses because they demonstrate research, speak to real pain, and make asking a tiny, easy yes.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name], a [your role] who helps [target customer type] with [the specific outcome you deliver]. My best clients are [2-3 characteristics of ideal clients].

PROSPECT INFO:
- Name: [First name]
- Title: [Their title]
- Company: [Company name]
- What they do: [Brief description]
- Why I'm reaching out to them specifically: [What triggered this outreach? Recent news, a post they made, a mutual connection, a specific problem they likely have?]
- Specific signal or reason: [The more specific, the better]

DELIVERABLE:
Write 3 versions of a cold email that I can A/B test. Each version should:

- Be under 120 words in the body (excluding signature)
- Have a subject line under 7 words
- Open with a pattern interrupt (not "I hope this finds you well")
- Identify a specific problem they likely have (based on their role/company)
- Reference one specific, credible proof point from my work
- Close with ONE specific, easy ask — a 15-minute call, a reply to one question, or a single yes/no answer
- Never use the words "synergy," "leverage," "circle back," "touch base," or "thought leader"

VERSION STRUCTURE:
- Version A: Lead with a specific observation about their business or recent activity
- Version B: Lead with a data point or industry insight relevant to their situation  
- Version C: Lead with a short, relevant case study (2 sentences max)

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS:
Write 3 subject line options for each version. Mark which one you'd test first.

MY PROOF POINTS:
[List 2-3 specific results you've achieved for clients, with numbers if possible. E.g., "Helped [type of company] increase proposal win rate from 23% to 47% in 90 days."]

TARGET ROLE: [The specific title/function you're reaching out to]
TARGET INDUSTRY: [Industry]
THEIR LIKELY PAIN: [What problem does someone in this role at this type of company typically struggle with?]

CONSTRAINTS:
- Every word must earn its place. If it doesn't help, cut it.
- The ask should create almost no friction — they should be able to reply in under 30 seconds
- Do not pitch your services. Create enough curiosity that they want to learn more.
- Write in first person as if you are me
```

## Follow-Up Sequence

After your first email, send this follow-up sequence. Run it through the same AI with this prompt:

```
Write a 3-email follow-up sequence for a cold email prospect who hasn't responded. 

The original email was about: [paste your first email]

Rules:
- Email 2 (3 days after Email 1): Add new value — a resource, insight, or angle they didn't see in email 1. Under 80 words.
- Email 3 (7 days after Email 2): Social proof — brief case study. Under 80 words.
- Email 4 (14 days after Email 3): The "breakup" email. Low-pressure, leaves the door open. Under 50 words.

Do not repeat yourself across emails. Each one should stand alone and add something new.
```

## What Makes Cold Email Work

**The 3 rules that matter:**

1. **Specificity beats personalization.** "I saw your post about [specific thing]" beats "I've been following your work."

2. **One ask, not multiple options.** Giving them choices kills response rates. "Are you open to a 15-minute call this week or next?" gets more replies than "Let me know if you'd like to discuss."

3. **Short is not about length — it's about density.** 80 words that are all signal outperforms 200 words with any filler.
