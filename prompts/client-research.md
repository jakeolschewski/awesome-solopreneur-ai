# Client Research Prompt

Use this prompt before any sales call, discovery session, or first meeting with a prospect. The better your research, the more confident you show up — and the faster you earn trust.

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are acting as a senior business researcher preparing me for an important client meeting.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name/role], a [consultant/freelancer/service provider] who specializes in [your specialty]. I have an upcoming call with [prospect's name] at [company name]. They reached out because [reason they contacted you / pain point mentioned].

DELIVERABLE:
Prepare a comprehensive pre-call research brief I can review in 10 minutes. Format it as:

1. COMPANY SNAPSHOT (4-5 sentences)
   - What they do, who they serve, business model, approximate size
   - Recent news, funding, leadership changes, or pivots

2. LIKELY PAIN POINTS
   - 3-5 business challenges common to companies like this
   - Specific signals from their website, LinkedIn, or job postings that suggest what's broken

3. THEIR COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
   - Who their main competitors are
   - Where they appear to be winning or losing vs competitors

4. SMART QUESTIONS TO ASK
   - 5 discovery questions that demonstrate business acumen (not generic "what keeps you up at night" questions)
   - Order them from most important to least important

5. MY POSITIONING HOOK
   - One sentence that connects my specialty ([your specialty]) to their most likely pain point
   - This should feel tailored, not boilerplate

6. RED FLAGS TO WATCH
   - Any signals from their public presence that suggest they may be a difficult client, price-sensitive, or not ready to buy

CONSTRAINTS:
- Write everything from the perspective of someone who has done this research manually, not as a list of search suggestions
- Be specific to [company name], not generic
- Keep total length under 600 words — I need to absorb this quickly
- Flag anything you're uncertain about clearly

COMPANY TO RESEARCH:
- Name: [Company Name]
- Website: [URL]
- LinkedIn: [URL if available]
- Industry: [Industry]
- Contact: [Contact Name, Title]
```

---

## How to Use This Prompt

**Before the call:** Run this in Claude or ChatGPT with web search enabled. Give it the company's URL and LinkedIn profile to maximize specificity.

**Supplement it:** After running the prompt, quickly scan their LinkedIn page yourself, Google their name + "review" or "testimonial," and check Glassdoor if they have employees.

**During the call:** Keep the research brief in a split-screen or printed. Reference specific points naturally — "I noticed you recently [X], what's driving that?" — it signals you've done your homework without being robotic about it.

**Best AI tools for this prompt:** Claude with web search, Perplexity (for the research portion), ChatGPT with browsing enabled.

---

## Variables to Customize

| Variable | Replace With |
|----------|-------------|
| `[your name/role]` | Your name and how you introduce yourself professionally |
| `[your specialty]` | What you specifically do (e.g., "B2B content marketing for SaaS companies") |
| `[prospect's name]` | The person's name |
| `[company name]` | The company you're researching |
| `[reason they contacted you]` | Why they reached out or the context for this meeting |
