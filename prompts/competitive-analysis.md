# Competitive Analysis Prompt

Knowing your competitors better than they know themselves is a sustainable edge. This prompt produces actionable competitive intelligence, not a feature comparison table.

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are a competitive intelligence analyst helping a solopreneur understand their market landscape and identify strategic opportunities.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name], a [your role] who offers [your services/products] to [your target market]. My primary value proposition is [what makes you different]. 

I want to understand the competitive landscape for [your market/category] and specifically analyze [Competitor A], [Competitor B], and [Competitor C].

DELIVERABLE:
Produce a competitive analysis with the following sections:

1. MARKET LANDSCAPE OVERVIEW (3-4 sentences)
   Describe the overall category: How crowded is it? Where is it growing? What's the primary battleground (price, service quality, specialization, speed)?

2. FOR EACH COMPETITOR, ANALYZE:

   a. POSITIONING: How do they describe themselves? What customer problem do they claim to solve? What's their elevator pitch?
   
   b. IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE: Who are they actually serving? (Based on their case studies, testimonials, language, and pricing)
   
   c. PRICING STRUCTURE: What do they charge and how? (Hourly, project, retainer, SaaS) — note where they're transparent vs opaque
   
   d. STRENGTHS: What are they genuinely good at? Why do customers choose them?
   
   e. WEAKNESSES: Where do they have gaps, complaints, or vulnerabilities? (Check G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Reddit, and their own reviews)
   
   f. MESSAGING GAPS: What problems do their customers have that their marketing doesn't address?

3. WHITESPACE OPPORTUNITIES (most important section)
   Based on this analysis, identify 3-5 specific positions in this market that are underserved or poorly served. For each:
   - What customer segment is being underserved?
   - Why are current players not serving them well?
   - What would a solution positioned for this segment look like?

4. MY COMPETITIVE EDGE
   Given my positioning ([your value proposition]) and my target market ([your target market]), where do I have a genuine, defensible advantage over each competitor? Be honest — if I don't have one somewhere, say so.

5. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS
   3 specific actions I should take in the next 90 days based on this analysis. Make them concrete, not strategic platitudes.

COMPETITORS TO ANALYZE:
- Competitor A: [Name + URL]
- Competitor B: [Name + URL]  
- Competitor C: [Name + URL]

MY CONTEXT:
- My specialty: [What you do]
- My target market: [Who you serve]
- My differentiator: [What makes you different]
- My price range: [Approximate pricing]
- My current positioning: [How you describe yourself]

CONSTRAINTS:
- Be specific. Generic observations ("they have good customer service") are not useful.
- Flag where you're uncertain or working from limited data.
- Don't soften bad news — if a competitor is genuinely better at something, say so clearly.
- Focus on strategic insights, not feature comparison tables.
```

## How to Supplement AI Research

AI analysis of competitors is fast but limited to public information. Augment it with:

**Free research:**
- Search "[Competitor] reviews site:reddit.com" — Reddit gives you unfiltered customer opinions
- Check G2.com, Capterra, or Trustpilot for their reviews sorted by "most recent"
- Read the negative reviews carefully — complaints reveal unmet needs
- Subscribe to their email list and watch how they communicate for 30 days
- Check LinkedIn for their employee count trend (growing = strong, shrinking = struggling)

**Paid research:**
- [SimilarWeb](https://similarweb.com) — traffic estimates, traffic sources, top pages
- [Semrush](https://semrush.com) — their keyword rankings and organic traffic
- [SpyFu](https://spyfu.com) — what keywords they're bidding on (signals intent + messaging)

## Turning This Into Action

After you have your analysis, run this follow-up prompt:

```
Based on the competitive analysis above, write a positioning statement for my business that:
1. Is clearly differentiated from [Competitor A, B, C]
2. Speaks to an underserved segment or need
3. Is believable given my actual capabilities
4. Can be expressed in one sentence

Then write 3 variations of my positioning for:
- My website homepage headline
- My LinkedIn headline
- How I'd answer "What do you do?" at a networking event
```
