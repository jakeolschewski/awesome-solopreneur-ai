# Meeting Prep Prompt

The quality of your questions determines the quality of your outcomes. This prompt helps you walk into any meeting knowing exactly what you need to learn, say, and decide.

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are an executive coach helping me prepare for an important business meeting.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name], a [your role]. I have an upcoming [meeting type: discovery call / project kickoff / check-in / renegotiation / difficult conversation / pitch] with [person's name], [their title] at [company].

MEETING BACKGROUND:
- How we know each other: [existing client / new prospect / referral / cold outreach / etc.]
- What's been discussed previously: [Brief summary of prior conversations or none]
- What's at stake: [What is the potential value of this relationship or this meeting?]
- What I want to happen as a result of this meeting: [Your specific desired outcome]
- What they likely want from this meeting: [Their perspective, as best you can guess]
- Any tension or complexity: [Anything awkward, unresolved, or potentially contentious]

DELIVERABLE:
Prepare a complete meeting prep brief with:

1. MY OBJECTIVE (one clear sentence)
   What is the single outcome that would make this meeting a success? Not multiple outcomes — the ONE thing.

2. THEIR LIKELY AGENDA
   Based on their role and our history, what do they want out of this meeting? What are they coming in prepared to discuss or decide?

3. QUESTIONS I MUST ASK (ranked by priority)
   - 3-5 questions I need answered to achieve my objective
   - For each question: Why it matters and what I'll do with the answer
   - Flag which question should come first, and which should come last

4. QUESTIONS THEY'RE LIKELY TO ASK ME
   - 3-5 questions I should be prepared to answer
   - For each: A suggested response that's honest, concise, and strategic

5. KEY MESSAGES I NEED TO LAND
   - 2-3 specific points I need them to understand or remember after this meeting
   - Framed in their interests, not mine

6. POTENTIAL OBJECTIONS AND HOW TO HANDLE THEM
   - What concerns might they raise?
   - For each: A prepared response that acknowledges their concern and moves forward

7. DECISION POINTS
   - What decisions need to be made in this meeting (if any)?
   - What's my position on each? What's my walk-away point?

8. MEETING OPENER
   Write the first 30 seconds of what I'll say to open this meeting and set the right tone

9. CLOSE AND NEXT STEPS
   What specific next steps should we agree on before ending the call? Write the exact words I'll use to close.

ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
[Paste any relevant emails, notes, their website, LinkedIn bio, or anything else that helps]

CONSTRAINTS:
- Be direct. If there's a likely awkward moment, name it and give me language for it.
- Prioritize ruthlessly — I can only focus on a few things. Don't give me a 20-point checklist.
- Write any sample language in a voice that sounds like me, not like a corporate script.
```

## For Specific Meeting Types

### Discovery Call
Add to your prompt:
```
This is a first meeting with a new prospect. I'm trying to determine:
1. Whether this is a qualified opportunity worth pursuing
2. What their real problem is beneath what they said they want
3. Whether we'd work well together

Add a section: QUALIFICATION CRITERIA — what 5 signals would tell me this is a strong opportunity vs. one I should pass on?
```

### Renegotiation or Difficult Conversation
Add to your prompt:
```
This is a potentially sensitive conversation. I need to raise [the issue] without damaging the relationship.

Add a section: CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK — the specific sequence I should use to raise this issue, with sample language for:
- Opening the topic
- Stating my position
- Inviting their perspective
- Reaching an agreement (or agreeing to next steps)
```

### Project Kickoff
Add to your prompt:
```
This is a kickoff meeting for a new engagement. I need to align on:
- Scope and deliverables
- Communication preferences and cadence
- Decision-making and approval processes
- Success metrics

Add a section: ALIGNMENT QUESTIONS — what I need to know in the first meeting to set this project up for success.
```

## After the Meeting

Use this quick follow-up prompt:

```
I just had a meeting with [person] at [company]. Here are my notes: [paste your notes]

Write:
1. A follow-up email (under 150 words) that: confirms what we agreed, summarizes key points, states clear next steps
2. 3 action items I need to complete (with suggested deadlines)
3. What I learned that I didn't know before this meeting
4. Whether this meeting moved my objective forward, and what I should do next
```
