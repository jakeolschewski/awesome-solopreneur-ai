# SEO Article Writing Prompt

An article that ranks drives compounding revenue. An article that doesn't rank is a cost. This prompt helps you write articles that earn traffic — not just word count.

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are an expert SEO content strategist and writer who specializes in creating articles for solopreneurs and small business owners. You understand both search engine optimization and what makes content genuinely useful to business professionals.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name], a [your role] at [your business]. My website is [URL]. I create content to attract [target audience] who are interested in [your topic area].

ARTICLE DETAILS:
- Primary keyword: [exact keyword or phrase you want to rank for]
- Secondary keywords: [2-4 related terms]
- Search intent: [What is someone looking for when they search this keyword? Are they trying to learn, compare options, or buy?]
- My reader: [Specific description — "a freelance graphic designer with 2-5 years experience who charges hourly and wants to move to project-based pricing"]
- Target word count: [1,500 / 2,000 / 2,500 / 3,000 words]
- Competitors to outrank: [Paste the URLs of 2-3 articles currently ranking for this keyword]

MY UNIQUE ANGLE:
[What perspective, experience, or information do I have that the existing ranking articles don't? If you don't have one, describe what the existing articles are missing.]

DELIVERABLE:
Write a complete, SEO-optimized article that:

1. RESEARCH FIRST:
   Analyze the search intent behind [primary keyword]. What does someone searching this actually want? What type of content are they expecting (listicle, how-to, comparison, analysis)?

2. ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
   - Title (H1): Include primary keyword, under 60 characters, compelling enough to click
   - Meta description: 140-155 characters, include primary keyword, describe the benefit of reading
   - Introduction (150-200 words): Hook + problem statement + what they'll learn. Primary keyword in first 100 words.
   - Body sections (H2s and H3s): Cover the topic comprehensively. Use secondary keywords naturally.
   - Conclusion (100-150 words): Summary + clear next step or CTA
   
3. SEO REQUIREMENTS:
   - Primary keyword appears in: title, first paragraph, at least 2 H2s, conclusion
   - Secondary keywords appear naturally throughout
   - At least one relevant internal link opportunity flagged with [INTERNAL LINK: topic]
   - At least 2 opportunities for external links to authoritative sources flagged with [EXTERNAL LINK: topic]
   - FAQ section at the end with 3-5 questions (targets featured snippets)

4. CONTENT REQUIREMENTS:
   - Include at least one original framework, model, or checklist that can't be found in competing articles
   - Use real examples, not hypothetical ones (mark where I should insert my own examples)
   - At least 2 places where I can insert a personal story or client example [STORY INSERT: topic]
   - No filler content — every section should answer a specific question the reader would have

5. FORMATTING:
   - Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
   - Bold the key insight in each section
   - Include at least one table or comparison if the topic supports it
   - One clear CTA (what should the reader do next?)

WRITING STYLE:
- Direct and practical, not academic
- First-person where appropriate
- Write for a [beginner / intermediate / advanced] audience
- Avoid: "In conclusion," "In today's digital landscape," "It's important to note," and any other filler phrases

CONSTRAINTS:
- Don't pad to hit word count — every paragraph should add value
- If the article needs to be long to be comprehensive, make it long. If not, don't force length.
- Flag any statistics or claims that I should verify before publishing with [VERIFY: claim]
```

## Before You Write: Keyword Validation

Before investing time in an article, validate the keyword:

```
I'm considering writing an article targeting the keyword: [keyword]

Help me evaluate whether this is worth targeting:
1. What is the likely monthly search volume range? (High / Medium / Low)
2. How difficult is this keyword to rank for? (High / Medium / Low — based on the sites currently ranking)
3. What is the search intent? (Informational / Navigational / Commercial / Transactional)
4. Is this a good keyword for a solopreneur website with modest domain authority?
5. What related keywords should I also target in this article?
6. What's the most likely format for ranking content? (How-to, listicle, comparison, etc.)

Competing URLs currently ranking: [paste URLs]
My website: [URL]
My domain authority estimate: [Low / Medium / High]
```

---

## Post-Writing Optimization

After your article is drafted, run this optimization check:

```
Review this article for [keyword] and suggest improvements:

1. WHERE should I add [keyword] or [secondary keyword] more naturally?
2. WHAT is the weakest section that adds the least value? How would you improve it?
3. ARE there any questions a searcher would have that this article doesn't answer?
4. WHAT snippet format (paragraph, list, table) should I optimize for featured snippet?
5. IS the introduction compelling enough to prevent someone from bouncing?

Article: [paste your draft]
```
