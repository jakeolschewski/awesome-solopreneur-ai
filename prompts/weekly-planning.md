# Weekly Planning Prompt

The difference between a solopreneur who grows and one who stays stuck is rarely effort — it's clarity about what to work on. This prompt designs your week before it designs itself.

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are an executive coach and productivity strategist helping me plan a high-impact week as a solopreneur.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name], a [your role]. My primary goal this quarter is [your 90-day goal]. This week specifically, I want to make progress on [what matters most this week].

MY CURRENT SITUATION:
- Active clients: [List client engagements and what's due or needed this week]
- Active projects: [List non-client projects — content, products, business development]
- Revenue goal this month: [$X] — I'm currently at [$Y] with [days] days left
- Outstanding follow-ups: [Anyone I need to reach out to this week?]
- Scheduled meetings: [List meetings already on the calendar]

TIME AVAILABLE:
- Hours available for focused work: [X hours]
- Days with meetings (and how many hours meetings take): [e.g., "Tuesday has 3 calls, total 3 hours"]
- Personal constraints this week: [Any appointments, travel, etc.]

DELIVERABLE:
Design my week with the following:

1. THE THEME FOR THIS WEEK (one sentence)
   Based on my goals and situation, what is the single most important thing this week should accomplish?

2. THE THREE WINS
   What are the 3 specific outcomes — not tasks — that would make this week objectively successful?
   These should be outcome-oriented, not activity-oriented.
   Bad: "Work on client proposal"
   Good: "Send final proposal to [client] by Thursday"

3. DAILY PLAN (Monday through Friday)
   For each day:
   - DEEP WORK BLOCK: What is the most important project to focus on? (This gets your best hours — before email, before meetings)
   - MEETINGS/CALLS: What's scheduled and what's the preparation needed?
   - ADMIN WINDOW: What lightweight tasks (email, invoicing, follow-ups) can be batched here?
   - END OF DAY REVIEW: What does "done" look like for today?

4. THIS WEEK'S REVENUE MOVES
   Based on my revenue situation, what 1-3 specific actions this week could directly generate or protect revenue?
   These are non-negotiable activities.

5. ENERGY MANAGEMENT
   - Which days should I schedule demanding client work?
   - Which days should I do creative or writing work?
   - Where should I protect recovery time?

6. WHAT TO DEFER OR DELETE
   Based on everything I've shared, what should I NOT work on this week even if it feels urgent?

7. THE WEEKLY REVIEW SETUP
   Write the 5 questions I should answer at the end of this week (Friday) to set myself up for a better next week.

CONSTRAINTS:
- Be ruthlessly specific. "Do business development" is not a plan. "Send 3 follow-up emails to prospects who went quiet" is.
- Protect at least one 2-hour deep work block per day — no meetings before 10am if possible
- Account for the fact that meetings cost more than their calendar time (prep + recovery)
- Revenue activities come before content activities, which come before admin
- If I've given you more than I can realistically do this week, tell me and help me prioritize

ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- What I'm anxious about this week: [Optional — what's weighing on you?]
- What I'm excited about this week: [Optional]
- What derailed me last week: [Optional — what got in the way?]
```

## Sunday Night Ritual (5-minute version)

For weeks when you don't need a full plan, use this quick version:

```
I have 5 minutes to plan my week. Here's my situation:

Top 3 must-accomplish outcomes this week:
1. [X]
2. [Y]  
3. [Z]

Meetings already scheduled: [list]
Available hours for focused work: [X hours across X days]

Give me:
1. The single most important thing to do Monday morning (before checking email)
2. One thing to defer until next week
3. The question I should ask myself at the end of each day to stay on track
```

## Weekly Review Prompt (Friday EOD)

```
Help me review this week and set up next week:

This week I planned to accomplish: [your three wins from Monday]

What actually happened:
- Wins: [What went well?]
- Misses: [What didn't get done or went poorly?]
- Surprises: [What came up that you didn't plan for?]

Questions to answer:
1. What made my most productive moments productive? (Protect these conditions next week)
2. What's still on my plate that needs to move forward?
3. What should I communicate to a client or prospect this week that I haven't yet?
4. What's the ONE thing that would make next week clearly better than this one?
5. Is my business growing, holding, or declining? What does the trend look like?

Based on my review, help me write the top 3 priorities for next week — and identify one thing I should say no to next week.
```

## Time Block Templates

Different types of weeks require different structures. Use this to customize:

```
I have a [delivery-heavy / business development / content creation / travel] week coming up.

Design a time block template appropriate for this type of week that:
- Protects my most important work type
- Accounts for the unique demands of this week type
- Helps me avoid the typical pitfalls of [week type] weeks

My working hours: [X to Y]
My location this week: [office / home / travel]
```
