# Case Study Writing Prompt

Case studies are your most powerful sales tool — more convincing than any testimonial, more specific than any pitch. A great case study makes your next prospect think "that's exactly my situation."

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are a business writer who specializes in turning client success stories into compelling case studies that win new business.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name], a [your role] at [your business name]. I want to write a case study about a successful client engagement to use on my website, in proposals, and in sales conversations.

CLIENT ENGAGEMENT DETAILS:
- Client description: [Industry, company size, role of main contact — anonymize if needed]
- The situation before working with me: [What was happening? What was broken, missing, or painful?]
- What they had tried before: [What had they already attempted? Why did it not work?]
- Why they hired me: [What made them reach out? What were they hoping for?]
- What I did (the work): [Describe the engagement — what you did, in what order, and why]
- What was hard or unexpected: [What obstacles came up? How did you handle them?]
- The results: [Quantify as much as possible — revenue, time saved, cost reduced, growth achieved, risk eliminated]
- The client's reaction: [Do you have a quote? What did they say about the experience?]
- What they're doing now: [Are they a repeat client? Did they refer others?]

CAN I NAME THE CLIENT? [Yes / No, use a disguised description]

DELIVERABLE:
Write a complete case study in two formats:

FORMAT A: FULL CASE STUDY (for website and proposals)
Structure:
- Headline (the result, not the process — e.g., "How a Solo Consultant Tripled Her Client Roster in 6 Months")
- The Situation (2-3 paragraphs: who is the client, what was wrong, why it mattered)
- The Challenge (1-2 paragraphs: what made this hard, what they'd already tried)
- The Approach (2-3 paragraphs: what I did and why — not a list of activities, a story of decisions)
- The Results (1 paragraph + 3-5 bullet points with specific numbers)
- What's Next (1 paragraph: what the client is working on now, ongoing relationship)
- Client Quote (real or suggested phrasing based on the story — mark clearly if suggested)

Length: 500-800 words

FORMAT B: MINI CASE STUDY (for proposals and LinkedIn)
Structure:
- One sentence: who the client is (described without naming)
- One sentence: what was broken
- 2-3 sentences: what I did
- One sentence: the result (the number)
- Optional client quote

Length: 100-150 words

VOICE GUIDELINES:
- Write in third person ("Jacob worked with..." not "I worked with...")
- Lead every section with the client's story, not my work
- Specific beats general every time: "increased proposal win rate from 23% to 47%" not "dramatically improved close rates"
- Avoid: "best-in-class," "holistic," "synergy," "leverage," "transformative"

CONSTRAINTS:
- If I can't provide a number, use a qualitative before/after instead
- Every paragraph should move the story forward
- The reader should finish this thinking "I have this same problem" not "what a great consultant"
```

## Getting the Story from Your Client

Use this prompt to prepare for a 20-minute client interview:

```
I'm about to interview a past client for a case study. The engagement was about: [brief description].

Write 10 interview questions that will get me the specific details, quotes, and numbers I need for a compelling case study. Focus on:
- The emotional experience (not just the facts)
- Specific moments where things changed
- Quantifiable outcomes they can verify
- Their perspective on what made the engagement work

Format them in the order I should ask them.
```

---

## Distributing Your Case Study

After writing your case study, create derivative assets:

```
I have a case study about [brief description]. 

Using this case study as source material, create:
1. A LinkedIn post (250 words) that tells the story with a lesson
2. A 3-slide carousel outline (hook, story, result)
3. A 2-sentence mention I can use in cold emails: "Recently I helped [client type described generically] achieve [result]. Here's how."
4. A version for my proposals under the heading "Relevant Experience"
5. A Twitter/X thread (lead tweet + 4 follow-up tweets)

Source case study: [paste it here]
```
