# Pricing Strategy Prompt

Most solopreneurs undercharge — not because they lack confidence, but because they're pricing based on cost and time instead of value and alternatives. This prompt fixes that.

---

## The WEDGE Prompt

```
You are a pricing strategist who specializes in helping independent consultants and freelancers price their services for maximum revenue and retention.

CONTEXT:
I am [your name], a [your role] who provides [specific service(s)] to [target market]. 

MY CURRENT SITUATION:
- Current pricing: [What I charge now, and how — hourly / project / retainer]
- Typical client type: [Describe your most common client]
- Average engagement size: [Dollar amount]
- Capacity: [How many clients can I handle at once?]
- Business goal: [Growth / stability / premium positioning / something else]

CLIENT OUTCOMES:
[Describe 2-3 specific results you've delivered for clients. Be as quantitative as possible.
E.g., "Helped a $2M consulting firm increase proposal win rate from 22% to 41%"
Or "Reduced a client's content production time by 60% while doubling their LinkedIn engagement"]

COMPETITOR PRICING (if known):
[What do others offering similar services charge? List 2-3 data points if you have them]

DELIVERABLE:
Build me a complete pricing strategy with the following:

1. PRICING PHILOSOPHY (2-3 sentences)
   What pricing model makes most sense for my service type and target market? Why?
   Options to evaluate: hourly, day rate, project-based, retainer, value-based, productized.
   Recommend one primary model with a secondary option.

2. VALUE BASELINE (the calculation that anchors everything)
   Based on my client outcomes, what is the economic value of what I deliver? Calculate:
   - Conservative case: the minimum verifiable value
   - Realistic case: the expected average value
   - Upside case: the exceptional result
   
   Then suggest what percentage of that value I should capture as my fee (and why).

3. PRICING TIERS
   Design 3 service tiers appropriate for my market:
   
   TIER 1 — Entry (for prospects who aren't ready for full engagement):
   - What's included
   - Price
   - Who it's for
   - How it leads to Tier 2
   
   TIER 2 — Core (your primary offering, majority of revenue):
   - What's included
   - Price
   - Who it's for
   - How you deliver it
   
   TIER 3 — Premium (your best clients, highest value):
   - What's included
   - Price
   - Who it's for
   - What makes it worth the premium

4. HOW TO PRESENT PRICING (the sales conversation)
   - At what point in a conversation should I introduce price?
   - How should I frame the options so Tier 2 feels like the right choice?
   - How do I respond when someone says "That's more than we expected"?
   - Word-for-word language for my 3 most common pricing conversations

5. ANNUAL REVENUE MODEL
   Given my capacity and this pricing structure, build a simple model:
   - If I have X clients at Tier 2, what's my revenue?
   - What mix of Tier 1/2/3 clients would hit my revenue goal of $[amount]?
   - What's my effective hourly rate at each tier?

6. RAISING MY PRICES
   Based on what I've told you, should I raise my prices? If yes:
   - How much?
   - How do I communicate it to existing clients?
   - Script the email I'd send to notify current clients of a price increase

CONSTRAINTS:
- Don't recommend prices without explaining the reasoning
- Be specific to my market, not generic
- If my current pricing is significantly below market, say so directly
- Include at least one "do this immediately" tactical recommendation
```

## Pricing Self-Audit Questions

Run through these with AI or on your own before using the main prompt:

1. When was the last time a prospect said yes immediately without negotiating? (If this happens often, you're underpriced.)
2. What is the most a client has ever paid you? What made that possible?
3. If you doubled your rate tomorrow, how many of your current clients would stay?
4. What does a similar outcome cost from an agency, a FTE hire, or the top 3 options in your market?
5. What would you need to charge to work with 3 clients instead of 8?

## The Productized Service Option

If you want to explore productized pricing, add this to your prompt:

```
Also design a productized version of my service where:
- The scope is fixed and clearly defined
- The price is fixed (no quotes, no negotiations)  
- The delivery timeline is fixed
- The client knows exactly what they're getting

This should be positioned as: [a starter offer / a standalone product / a step before a full engagement]

Include: the product name, the 3-sentence description I'd use on my website, the price, and the delivery process.
```
